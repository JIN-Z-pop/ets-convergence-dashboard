Key Insights

5A. Price Convergence Pressure: CBAM creates an economic incentive for Asian ETS to raise carbon prices toward EU levels. However, the 10x gap ($8-11 vs $79) means convergence will be gradual — likely reaching $25-40 range by 2030 for Korea/China.

5B. Institutional Convergence: All 4 systems are moving toward BM-based allocation (away from grandfathering). MRV standards are harmonizing around ISO 14064 / IPCC guidelines. This is the strongest convergence axis.

5C. Structural Divergence: The absolute cap (EU/Korea) vs intensity cap (China) divide remains fundamental. China's GDP-linked cap means total emissions can still rise. Japan's voluntary approach adds another paradigm.

5D. CBAM as Bridge: CBAM is the single most powerful convergence force. It effectively exports EU carbon pricing to trading partners. Korea and China are responding with ETS reforms, but the speed and depth of reform varies significantly.
